2 Method developments

Ab initio electronic structures methods provide a powerful tool to investigate the electronic structures and magnetic interactions in materials of interest. We calculate bare and full susceptibilities to describe magnetic interactions and spin excitations in various materials.

Tight-binding code is developed to investigate the detailed band structures and magnetic properties such as magnetocrystalline anisotropy. We start from the realistic TB model constructed using wannier90 or non-orthogonal basis and construct Hamiltonain and Green's functions. Some of the functions are listed below:

Spinwave theory, linear or nonlinear, is used in our research to compare with inelastic neutron scattering measurements or estimate Curie temperatures of 2D materials. One of the latter examples is our using of renormalized spin wave theory on magnetic 2D van der Waals materials. We also use to investigate the disorder effects on spin excitatiosn.

Micromagnetics is used to investigate extrinsic magnetic properties in permanent magnets or combined with string method to investigate the growth mechanism of magnetic skyrmions.
