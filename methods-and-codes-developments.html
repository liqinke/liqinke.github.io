2 Methods and codes developments

Ab initio linear response methods provide a powerful tool to investigate the electronic structures and magnetic interactions in materials of interest. We calculate bare and full dynamic spin susceptibilities to describe magnetic interactions and spin excitations in various materials. This method is useful for investating the magnetic interactions and excitations in itinerant systems, since the Stoner excitations and spinwave excitations are treated on equal footing.

Tight-binding code is developed to investigate the detailed band structures and magnetic properties such as magnetocrystalline anisotropy. Spinwave theory, linear or nonlinear, is used in our research to compare with inelastic neutron scattering measurements or estimate Curie temperatures of 2D materials. One of the latter examples is our using of renormalized spin wave theory on magnetic 2D van der Waals materials. We also use to investigate the disorder effects on spin excitatiosn.

Micromagnetics is used to investigate extrinsic magnetic properties in permanent magnets or combined with string method to investigate the growth mechanism of magnetic skyrmions.
