1.2 Topological materials

Two-dimensional (2D) Kagome-lattice of \(3d\) systems harbor Dirac crossings (DCs), flat bands, and electron correlations. In the intrinsic ferromagnetic (FM) Kagome lattice, the spin-orbit coupling (SOC) and the breaking of time-reversal symmetry introduce a gap at Dirac points, resulting in Chern gapped insulators. When these topological electronic states are near the Fermi level, large Berry curvatures are manifested, resulting in novel quantum properties such as the quantum anomalous Hall effect (QAHE).
