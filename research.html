0.2 Magnetic topological materials

I completed my Ph.D. at Arizona state university in 2009. My research interests lie in the fields of electronic structure methods and magnetism, with a current focus on electron correlations and spin excitations in 2D materials, magnetic topological materials, and other quantum materials.

Recent research interests:

  • Develop and apply ab initio dynamic spin susceptibility to describe magnetic interactions and spin excitations npj Comput Mater 7, 4 (2021).
  • Develop a highly efficient Green’s-function-based ab initio framework to analyze spin exchange and two-ion magnetocrystalline anisotropy Phys. Rev. B 99, 054418 (2019).
  • Besides numerical methods, I also enjoy analytical modeling, especially modeling of spin-orbit-coupling physics Phys. Rev. B, 92:014423 (2015).
